The Challenge: Balancing style with an efficient user experience

RESEARCH

One design element I noticed in his work is the use of lightweight structures and open frameworks, which give his buildings a sense of transparency and lightness, making it very experimental. He uses lots of natural materials in his designs like wood and glass, which builds a relationship between the natural world and the constructed environment. Additionally, the ideas of transparency, layering, and modularity are commonly explored in his works. His structures frequently have complex spatial layouts, where many volumes and levels overlap and interact.

ITERATIVE PROCESS

My objective was to create a simple website to make navigation as easy as possible while following the general design aesthetic of the actual artist himself. I shifted towards refining the placement of essential elements intended for consistency across all pages. I iterated on the design concepts based on feedback from users, focusing on enhancing usability, accessibility, and overall user satisfaction. Each iteration brought me closer to achieving the vision of a seamless and engaging user experience.

DESIGN FEATURES

  • Home Page - Features a curated selection of the architect's most notable projects, presented through a visually engaging hero area with high-quality images. The layout highlights standout works and includes interactive elements to enhance user engagement, providing an impactful introduction to the architect's portfolio.
  • Projects - Each image was linked to their corresponding projects, with name, location, year, and a short description. After making all project pages, I added arrows on each page for the user to cycle through all the projects. The user can then scroll through various images of that particular architect‚Äôs work.
  • About & Contact - Developing the 'About' and 'Contact' pages involved a structured approach aimed at maintaining consistency across the website. I began by focusing on aligning the headers identically to establish a cohesive visual identity. With the framework set, I organized the content, including paragraphs and images, adhering closely to the predefined margin guidelines implemented across all pages. This attention to detail not only ensures a seamless user experience but also reinforces the overarching design aesthetic, creating a cohesive portfolio.

VISUAL ANALYSIS

FEEDBACK SESSIONS

Throughout the development of this portfolio, I engaged in multiple rounds of feedback sessions, which provided invaluable insights and suggestions, including:

  • Footer Addition - Feedback suggested adding a footer. The implemented footer includes shortcuts to all pages, a "copyright" section, and a recruitment email subscription section.
  • Navigation Bar - Consider keeping the navigation bar fixed, as the unique menu icon is a significant feature of the site.
  • Menu Icon Color Changes - Implemented color changes for menu icon squares based on cursor location and the designer‚Äôs aesthetic.
  • Hover Animation in Projects Menu - The on-hover animation for project names was deemed unnecessary; changed to a hover effect that enlarges images instead.
  • Intersection Observer - Introduced to manage animations and lazy-loading of images, improving website performance and responsiveness. Replaced previous moving-down intro animation which caused conflicts with CSS code.
